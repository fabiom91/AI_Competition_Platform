What is an Article

In the scope of this web community, an article is meant to share a research effort or an experience related to Neonatology and/or Paediatrics. This is not a Peer Reviewed Journal although you are free to share your pubblication with the community and receive a feedback.

What is a Machine Learning (ML) Competition

A ML competition is a open challange to data scientists to develop a ML model or Statistical model to predict a given class feature. E.g. A research centre have collected a vast amount of data regarding a clinical condition but has no-one able to analyse those data and develop an alghoritm able to predict the new patients outcomes or is just interested in getting a feedback from other data scientists and/or statisticians.

What’s the difference between an Article and a Competition

An article is to share a share a research effort or an experience related to Neonatology and/or Paediatrics while a Competition require sharing some data and having some knowledge about Machine Learning and / or statistics to understand the results.

Why do I need to give a weight to an evaluation measure

The evaluation measure weight is used by our automated leaderboard alghoritm to give assign an overall score to each submission and populate a more accurate leaderboard. Is not really important what value you assign to a single evaluation measure but the difference of weight between different evaluation measure: If a competition has a single evaluation measure, that evaluation measure will be used as the sole measure to calculate each submission score regardless of the weight given. On the other hand, if multiple evaluation measure have been selected with different weight, the results obtained by a submission will be multiplied by the weight of the correspondent evaluation measure to calculate the overall score: 

# Recall valued more:
# (increase value of false positives)

weighted_scores = {
  'Accuracy' : 2,
  'Precision': 4,
  'Recall' : 8
}

submission_results = {
  'Accuracy' : 0.7,
  'Precision': 0.6,
  'Recall' : 0.2
}

overall_score = (0.7 * 2) + (0.6 * 4) + (0.2 * 8) = 5.4

# Precision valued more:
# (increase value of true positives)

weighted_scores = {
  'Accuracy' : 2,
  'Precision': 8,
  'Recall' : 4
}

submission_results = {
  'Accuracy' : 0.7,
  'Precision': 0.6,
  'Recall' : 0.2
}

overall_score = (0.7 * 2) + (0.6 * 8) + (0.2 * 4) = 7

How do I join a Competition

  1. click on a competition to open it
  2. scroll down and click on the Join Competition button

A popup window will inform you that you have joined the competition. You should now download the available files from the table above the Join Competition button and make your own model. When ready, you can submit your results by clicking on the Submit your Results button.

Your results should be in the format of a .csv spreadsheet. The csv file doesn’t necessarily have to contain all the features of the dataset you’ve downloaded as validation_set.csv or features you created or modified but you must label your results as “class” :

NB: In the example above, your submission is the predicted_class.csv file.

You must also upload a link to your code / notebook or repository online (e.g. github repository or google drive / dropbox archive).

Where are my personal data stored and who can access them

All your personal data are stored in a Firebase, Firestore Database located in London, UK and GDPR Compliant. Please review our Privacy Policy for more information.

The following information of your profile are publicly displayed:

  • your Name and Surname
  • your public email address and phone number
  • your qualifications and your motto
  • your badge and reputation
  • your articles / competitions written and saved
  • your competitions joined and the results of your submissions (the actual submissions is however available only to admin and the competition owner).

Where are my competition data stored and who can access them

In addition to the Firebase Firestore Database metioned in the question above, your competitions files (not the submissions) can also be stored securely on our server situated in University College Cork - UCC, Ireland and GDPR Compliant. Please review our Privacy Policy for more information. They can be accessed by the Server and the Platform administrators and partially by anyone on the platform except for the validation dataset true labels which are hidden to the public.

Where are my article resources stored and who can access them

Your articles resources are stored in a Firebase, Firestore Database located in London, UK and GDPR Compliant. Please review our Privacy Policy for more information. All your articles resources can be accessed by anyone on the Platform.

How do I cancel my account

You can cancel your account by sending a request via email at airesearch.platform@gmail.com. Your account will be cancelled immediately after you receive a confirmation email (it may take some days) and you’ll have other 15 days from the confirmation email, to restore your account. After this date, all your personal data, your articles, competitions and comments will be erased by our servers and we won’t be able to retreive them anymore.

If you think your account has been deleted without your permission, please, contact us immediately at: airesearch.platform@gmail.com
